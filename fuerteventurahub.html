Buying our first
property

First on our agenda was to establish a bolthole on the island which we can use as a holiday home. We first did our research on the internet to look for properties that we can afford. Our main priority was the location. As we have already been to the south of the island in Jandia for a holiday, we wanted to check out the north of the island, Corralejo. We concentrated our search on A Place In The Sun website where a lot of properties are being advertised, plus, we are huge fans of the show! We found quite a few estate agents whilst searching for properties but the one that really stood out for us was a company called Goldacre Estates. Luckily enough, they are based in Corralejo. Needless to say, we arranged an appointment with them to coincide with our trip to Corralejo. We viewed a couple of properties in Corralejo but as fate would have it, we ended up buying a 3 bed villa with a private pool and a roof terrace with seaviews in Costa De Antigua which is in the east near the capital city of Puerto Del Rosario and a 30 minute walk to a local beach Caleta De Fuste.

Get to Know Us

Hello again! I'm Grace, author of this blog and I am from the Philippines! I am a British/Filipino citizen and my husband Daniel is a British/Irish national. We first visited Fuerteventura in 2019 and instantly fell in love with it. The all year round sunshine and warm weather, the quality of life and the beautiful beaches amongst others reasons that really got us hooked by Fuertevetura. Since buying a property in 2021, we have been visiting every 6 to 8 weeks, mainly to explore more of the island but most importantly, to prepare for our planned relocation. With my husband being an Irish Citizen, our plan is to apply for his residency first - Tarjeta de Identification del Extranjero - TIE. Following that, our next plan of action is to apply for my EU family member residence card, which, at the time of writing this post, the EU family member residence card is valid for 5 years . The good news for me is that I only require 2 years of residency to be eligible to apply for Spanish Citizenship, all thanks to the Philippines being a Spanish colony many moons ago But that's neither here or now, so in the meantime, come and join us and let's take this journey together!
